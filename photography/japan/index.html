In Fall 2015, I took a trip to explore Japan – a place I've always wanted to visit since I started to study design. I spent a week with two friends, Sarah and Pascal, and two weeks alone. Here are some of the things that I saw during my stay there.

Ikebukuro

A commercial and entertainment district in Toshima, Tokyo, Japan. The district had a nice, energetic downtown and a quiet residential area which made for a great launching pad to explore Tokyo.

Walking through the rain-washed streets of Ikebukuro.

Shinjuku

A special ward located in Tokyo Metropolis, Japan. It's a major commercial and administrative centre, housing the busiest railway station in the world (Shinjuku Station) along with some of the best udon noodles I've ever had.

The outdoor patio of a Starbucks.

Ginza

A district of Chūō, Tokyo that is known for it's upscale shopping. This is a great place to find high-end retailers, unique boutiques, and my personal favorite: a 12-story UNIQLO.

A cool building in Ginza, the high-end shopping district of Tokyo.

Itoya, an eight-story store full of stationary.

Koppabashi-dori

Koppabashi-dori, or Kitchen Town, is a street in Tokyo which is full of restaurant supply stores. You can find everything from knives to plastic food used for restaurant display cases. You'll also find the best menchi-katsu I've ever had from a small shop.

Tokyo Tower seen from the kitchen district.

Kyoto

A city located in the central part of the island of Honshu, Japan. Formerly the Imperial capital of Japan for more than one thousand years, it is now the capital city of Kyoto Prefecture.

Scaffolding of Kyoto station.

Our Airbnb in Kyoto.

The Inari Temple. When you visit, go here at sunset.

Grab-n-go sushi! The food in Japan is always delicious.

Ginkaku-ji, the Golden Temple.

Ryōan-ji, a Zen temple in northwest Kyoto. There are 15 stones in the garden that require you to walk around to see them all.

Arashiyama

A district on the outskirts of Kyoto. It's known for its monkey park, bamboo forest, and the famous Moon Crossing Bridge on the Ōi River.

The Bamboo forest.

Osaka

A large port city and commercial center on the Japanese island of Honshu, is known for its modern architecture, boisterous nightlife and hearty street food.

In Nara, there are tame deer walking around. You can feed them with biscuits bought at street stands.

The largest Buddha statue in Western Japan. It's around 25 meters tall.

Okonomiyake, a special of Osaka, is a savory Japanese pancake.

Koya San

Situated in a plain atop Mount Koya, Koya San is home to an active monastic center founded centuries ago. I spent three days in a Buddhist Ryokan where I woke up early for meditation, drank tea while reading, and bathed in an onsen (hot spring).
