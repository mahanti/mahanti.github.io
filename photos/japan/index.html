--- layout: japan title: Japan ---

Ever since I started studying design, Japan has been a constant source of inspiration. From the culture to their immaculate attention to details, I knew I had to experience life there. Here are a few of the photos I took on my trip. It's hard to capture just how beautiful Japan is but I hope these give a glimpse into my trip.

Ikebukuro is a commercial and entertainment district in Toshima, Tokyo, Japan. The district had a nice, energetic downtown and a quiet residential area which made it a great launch pad to explore the rest of Tokyo.

Strolling through the rain-washed streets of Ikebukuro on my first morning in Tokyo.

Shinjuku is a special ward located in Tokyo Metropolis, Japan. It is a major commercial and administrative centre, housing the busiest railway station in the world (Shinjuku Station) along with some of the best udon noodles I've ever had.

The outdoor patio of a Starbucks.

The evening lights of Ikebukuro.

Shōchū, a sweet-potato based liquor. A clean, earthy taste.

The first of many yakitori trips featuring a rare tendon that connects the heart and liver.

Hanging out at one of the many arcades in Shinjuku.

We went to an owl cafe in Tokyo. Yes. That's right. An owl cafe.

Ginza is a district of Chūō, Tokyo that is known for it's upscale shopping. This is a great place to find high-end retailers, unique boutiques, and my personal favorite: a 12-story UNIQLO.

A cool building in Ginza, the high-end shopping district of Tokyo.

Itoya, an eight-story store full of stationary.

Koppabashi-dori, or Kitchen Town, is a street in Tokyo which is full of restaurant supply stores. You can find everything from knives to plastic food used for restaurant display cases. You'll also find the best menchi-katsu I've ever had from a small shop.

Tokyo Tower seen from the kitchen district.

Kyoto is a city located in the central part of the island of Honshu, Japan. Formerly the Imperial capital of Japan for more than one thousand years, it is now the capital city of Kyoto Prefecture.

Scaffolding of Kyoto station.

Our Airbnb in Kyoto.

The Inari Temple. When you visit, go here at sunset.

Grab-n-go sushi! The food in Japan is always delicious.

Ginkaku-ji, the Golden Temple.

Arashiyama is a district on the outskirts of Kyoto. It's known for its monkey park, bamboo forest, and the famous Moon Crossing Bridge on the Ōi River.

The Bamboo forest.

If you're at the right angle, the moon appears to cross the bridge as the night passes.
